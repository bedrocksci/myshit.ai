---
title: "Same knowledge, opposite owner"
summary: "An AI that knows you deeply is a personal assistant. Held by someone else, the same knowledge is surveillance. The difference is who holds it."
classification: public
date: 2026-04-24
---

Picture an AI that knows exactly who you are. What you do for a living. Who your friends, colleagues, and family are. What you wrote last week, what you were worrying about last month, what projects are in flight. How you talk. How you make decisions.

Take a second with that. What do you picture?

For some people it's a gift. A long-tenured assistant who starts every morning with a brief, drafts emails in your own voice, remembers the person you had coffee with six weeks ago and what you talked about. A chief of staff. A friend with perfect recall.

For other people, exactly the same description is the surveillance state. Palantir. Every database agency with a contract. Every corporate security team running behavioral analytics on its own employees.

The knowledge is identical. The difference is who holds it.

That is the entire question.

It is not a claim that any particular AI company is Palantir. It is a structural observation: "whoever holds the picture decides what the picture is used for" is a property of how the picture is stored, not of the current intentions of the people running the company.

Intentions change. Companies get acquired. Founders leave. New executives arrive with a different mandate. The business model rotates. Five years from now, the picture you built today will be sitting in a system somewhere, and someone you've never met will be making decisions about it that you never imagined signing up for.

The only version of this story where your answer stays yours is the one where the picture sits on your computer. Where you can read it. Where you decide, per conversation, what pieces to share with which tool.

Same depth. Same usefulness. Opposite owner.
