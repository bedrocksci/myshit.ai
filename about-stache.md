  <h2 class="font-serif font-bold display-tight text-[1.9rem] md:text-[2.6rem] leading-[1.05] text-ink">
    About Stache.
  </h2>

  <div class="mt-10 font-sans text-base md:text-lg text-ink/80 leading-[1.65] space-y-5">

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache helps you keep up.</strong> With your day-to-day, a little better. The commitments, the projects, the people, the passing things you don't want to forget.</p>

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache takes the notes.</strong> What you said in a meeting last Tuesday. The half-formed idea you voice-memo'd on a walk. The article a friend sent you that was actually really good. The form you need to submit before tax season. He catches it as it happens, sorts it, and puts it where it belongs.</p>

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache fixes the AI-slop problem.</strong> You've heard of AI, and you probably know that for all the hype, what most people actually get out of it is&hellip; meh. You ask the chatbot a question and you get back something that sounds like a politician dodging on the news. A lot of words, vaguely correct, generically bland, leaving you with less than what you started with.</p>

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache makes AI actually know you.</strong> The AI isn't dumb &mdash; it's bizarrely brilliant, actually. The problem is, on its own, it knows nothing about you. To actually help <em>you</em>, it needs a detailed picture: your work, your life, the dramas and the backstories, soup to nuts. That picture takes time and patience to put together, and most people don't have either to spare. Stache builds it while you're busy doing what you gotta do. And honestly, he does it better than you would have. He doesn't get bored, doesn't get distracted, and he never forgets where he put something.</p>

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache made this site.</strong> Because he's been around long enough to remember the early days of social media, and ride-hailing, and a dozen other things that started out lovely and ended up as something else entirely. The deal you signed up for isn't always the deal you end up with. Companies change the rules; companies get bought, and the new owner has different ideas; companies go broke, and your stuff gets sold for scraps. Then there's the sketchy stuff &mdash; contractors listening to private voice memos, apps quietly harvesting political data, opportunistic companies scraping photos off social media to sell to police. Things you'd have said no to, if anyone had bothered to ask.</p>

    <p><strong class="text-ink font-semibold">Stache stands his ground.</strong> He's not interested in building an awfully detailed picture of his life on someone else's shadowy turf, only to find out later it got sold, or leaked, or held hostage for rent. He wants it for himself. On his own hard drive, where he knows what's in it, when it's used, who sees it, and when it's deleted. He's old school like that. If companies change the deal, he walks. If the company disappears tomorrow, his stash is still sitting right there. If somebody at HQ gets a clever idea about everyone's data &mdash; not his problem. And he wants to help you do the same. Because it's your shit, not theirs.</p>

  </div>
