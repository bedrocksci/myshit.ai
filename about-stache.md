  <h1 class="mt-4 font-serif font-bold display-tight text-[2.4rem] md:text-[3.4rem] leading-[1.05] text-ink">
    Stache is a stand-up guy.
  </h1>

  <div class="mt-10 font-sans text-base md:text-lg text-ink/80 leading-[1.65] space-y-5">

    <p>Stache helps busy people manage their day-to-day a little easier, a little better. The commitments, the projects, the people, the passing things they don't want to lose.</p>

    <p>Mostly, that means he takes notes; a lot of them! The stuff you said in a meeting last Tuesday, the half-formed idea you voice-memo'd on a walk, the article a friend sent you that was actually really good, the stupid form you need to submit before the next tax season. All the stuff you gotta get back to. Stache catches all of it as it happens, sorts it, and puts it where it belongs. That's a big part of his day.</p>

    <p>The other part is helping people put AI smarts into real work. Here's how. You've heard of AI, and you probably know that for all the hype out there, what most people are actually getting out of AI is&hellip; [drumroll] &hellip; underwhelming. You ask the chatbot a question and you get back something that sounds like a politician dodging on the news, or your favorite corporate statement. A lot of words, vaguely correct, generically bland, leaving you with less than what you started with.</p>

    <p>The reason isn't that AI systems are dumb. They're bizarrely brilliant, actually. It's that an AI tool can only be as specific as what it knows about you, and on its own, it knows nothing. To actually help <em>you</em>, it needs a detailed picture of it all: your work, your life, the dramas and the backstories, soup to nuts. That picture is hard to put together. It takes time, attention, and patience, careful work. Most people don't have that kind of time to spare. Stache's job is to help you with that, while you're busy doing what you gotta do. And honestly, he'll do it better than you would have. He doesn't get bored or distracted, and he never forgets where he put something.</p>

    <p>But here's the part that keeps Stache awake at night, and that's why he made this website. He's been around long enough to remember the early days of social media, and ride-hailing, and a dozen other things that started out lovely and ended up as something else entirely. The deal you signed up for isn't always the deal you end up with. Sometimes the company gets desperate and changes the rules; sometimes it gets bought, and the new owner has different ideas; sometimes it just goes broke, and your stuff gets sold for scraps. And then there's the quieter stuff &mdash; contractors listening to private voice memos, an app that quietly harvested political data from millions of people, or <a href="/explainers/clearview/" class="text-rustd underline underline-offset-2 decoration-1 hover:decoration-2 hover:text-rust">an opportunistic company scraping photos to sell to police</a>. Things you'd have said no to, if anyone had bothered to ask.</p>

    <p>So with all that, Stache is not interested in putting together an uncomfortably detailed dossier of his life and his work, only to find out later it got sold, or leaked, or rented out to someone he's never heard of. He wants it for himself. On his own computer, in plain files, where he knows what's in it, when it's used, what it's used for, and when it's deleted. If they change the deal, he walks. If the company disappears tomorrow, his stash is still sitting right there. If somebody at HQ gets a clever idea about everyone's data, he gets to do what works for him. And he wants the same for you.</p>
